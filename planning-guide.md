# Jazz Fest Planning Guide - First Weekend 2026

## Daily Strategy

### General Tips
- **Arrive early** (11 AM gates) to stake out spots for evening headliners
- **Multiple stages run simultaneously** - you WILL have conflicts
- **Peak hours** (4-7 PM) have the biggest acts but also biggest crowds
- **Midday gems** (12-3 PM) often have easier viewing and great local talent
- **Food lines** are longest 12-2 PM - eat early or late
- **Parades happen throughout the day** - catch at least one Mardi Gras Indian parade

### Scheduling Conflicts to Consider

#### Thursday Evening (5-7 PM)
**Tough choices:**
- Kings of Leon (Festival, 5:00) vs. Stephen Marley (Congo Square, 5:25) vs. Monty Alexander (WWOZ, 5:45)
- Can potentially catch partial sets if stages are close

#### Friday Evening (5-7 PM)
**Major conflict:**
- Jon Batiste (5:10) vs. Lorde (5:30) - **different stages, overlapping times**
- Could potentially catch Jon Batiste then move to catch Lorde
- Sean Paul (Congo Square, 5:45) overlaps with both
- Hiromi's Sonicwonder (WWOZ, 5:45) is another option

#### Saturday Evening (5-7 PM)
**Biggest conflict day:**
- Stevie Nicks (Festival, 5:30) 
- Tyler Childers (Fais Do-Do, 5:20)
- Nas (Congo Square, 5:55)
- All three are huge draws at the same time

#### Sunday Evening (5-7 PM)
**Legendary closers:**
- Rod Stewart (5:25) vs. David Byrne (Congo Square, 5:30) vs. Jon Batiste Swamp (5:30)
- Different stages but all overlapping - can potentially catch parts of multiple sets
- The Isley Brothers (Congo Square, 5:40) also in mix

## Don't-Miss Experiences

### Cultural Essentials (unique to Jazz Fest)
1. **At least one Mardi Gras Indian parade** - Thursday 12:20 or Sunday 2:45 (Louis Armstrong celebration)
2. **A second line with Social Aid & Pleasure Clubs** - Friday or Saturday
3. **Traditional jazz in Economy Hall** - intimate, authentic
4. **Gospel Tent on Sunday morning** - powerful performances
5. **Pow Wow at Louisiana Folklife Village** - cultural exchange

### Food Strategy
**Must-try festival foods:**
- Crawfish Monica (the iconic dish)
- Cochon de Lait po-boy
- Soft shell crab po-boy
- Mango Freeze
- Crawfish bread

**Timing:**
- Eat before noon or after 2 PM to avoid peak lines
- Bring cash - most vendors prefer it
- Budget $15-25 per meal

### Logistics Between Stages
**Stage proximity clusters:**
- **Close together:** Festival Stage + Congo Square (easy to split sets)
- **Walkable:** Most stages are 5-10 min walk
- **Furthest:** Children's Tent and Gentilly Stage from main area
- **Plan 15 minutes** to move between distant stages during crowded times

## Group Coordination Tips

### Pre-Festival
- [ ] Review lineup and pick top 3-5 must-sees per person
- [ ] Identify conflicts and decide compromises
- [ ] Set meeting points for regroup times
- [ ] Exchange phone numbers (cell service can be spotty)
- [ ] Agree on meal times

### During Festival
- **Morning meetup** (11:30 AM) - review day plan
- **Flexibility:** OK to split up for different acts
- **Regroup times:** Between major acts (3 PM, 5 PM good checkpoints)
- **End-of-day meetup point:** Near exit to leave together

### Download/Print
- Jazz Fest official app (has real-time updates)
- This lineup guide
- Fair Grounds map (shows stage locations)

## Weather Contingencies
- **Rain:** Festival is rain or shine, bring ponchos
- **Heat:** Highs in mid-80s°F likely, sun protection essential
- **Hydration:** Water stations available, bring refillable bottle
