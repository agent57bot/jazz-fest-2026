## Core Principle: Loose Coordination, Not Military Operation

Jazz Fest with acquaintances works best with **structured flexibility**:
- Set **anchor points** (meals, meetups) but allow independence in between
- Don't try to keep everyone together all day
- Respect that people have different priorities

## Transportation Coordination

### To Fair Grounds (Morning)

**Option 1: Group Rideshare Carpool**
- Meet at Constantinople St or Quarter House at 10:30 AM
- Order 2-3 Uber XLs or Lyfts
- Split cost (~$8-12 per person total)
- Everyone arrives together

**Option 2: Independence**
- "Gates open at 11 AM, everyone get there however you want"
- Share tips (streetcar, rideshare pickup spots, etc.)
- Easier but less bonding

**Recommended:** Option 1 for Thursday (first day, group energy), Option 2 for Fri-Sun

### From Fair Grounds (Evening)

**DO NOT try to coordinate group departure** unless everyone commits to leaving at 5 PM (before closing).

**Why:** 
- Matt & Dawn (and others) want to stay through 7 PM closing
- Some may want to leave at 5-6 PM to beat traffic
- Coordinating 8-10 people in post-fest chaos = disaster

**Solution:** 
- "Everyone handles their own transport back. See you at dinner at 8 PM."
- Share the **transportation guide** (Morning Call strategy, streetcar, etc.)
- Let people self-organize into subgroups if they want

**Exception:** If 4+ people all want to leave at 5 PM, coordinate a shared rideshare for that subgroup.

## Managing Group Dynamics

### The Acquaintance Challenge

With people who don't know each other well:
- **Some will bond, some won't** - that's normal
- **Subgroups will form** - encourage it, don't fight it
- **Energy levels vary** - don't force all-day togetherness
- **Music taste differs** - that's why you split up at the fest

### Red Flags to Avoid

❌ **Over-scheduling** - "We're all seeing X at 2 PM, then Y at 3 PM, then..."  
✅ **Instead:** "Optional meetup at X at 2 PM if anyone's interested"

❌ **Forced togetherness** - "Everyone must stay together all day"  
✅ **Instead:** "Do your own thing, regroup for dinner"

❌ **Guilt-tripping no-shows** - "Where were you? We waited!"  
✅ **Instead:** "Missed you, but hope you had fun!"

❌ **Complex group decisions** - "Let's vote on every act"  
✅ **Instead:** "I'm seeing X, join me if you want"

### Making It Fun

## Laura's Sanity Preservation Tips

1. **You're the organizer, not the babysitter** - People are adults, they can handle themselves
2. **Set clear expectations upfront** - "Dinners are anchors, festival time is independent"
3. **Have one co-organizer** - Delegate transport or dinner planning to someone
4. **Build in alone time for yourself** - You don't have to attend every optional meetup
5. **Prepare for 20% flake rate** - Some people will skip plans, that's normal
6. **Have fun yourself** - Don't spend the whole fest coordinating

## Emergency Contingencies

**Someone gets separated/lost:**
- Meet at **Fair Grounds main entrance** (obvious landmark)
- Or **WWOZ booth** (central, everyone knows it)

**Someone feels overwhelmed/overstimulated:**
- Chill-out zones: Gospel Tent (peaceful), Folklife Village (quieter)
- OK to leave early and skip dinner

**Rain/weather:**
- Fest is rain or shine
- Ponchos, not umbrellas (umbrellas block views)
- Adjust dinner timing if everyone's soaked and wants to change

**Someone has too much to drink:**
- Buddy system back to accommodations
- Water stations throughout fest
- Don't let drunk person wander alone in NOLA
